## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Extraction

**Description:**
The extraction pass is a single-shot, LLM-backed structured-output call that reads one parsed Markdown document's sections and returns four categories of semantic information — concepts, glossary terms, entities, and references — as a validated Pydantic model. There is no end user directly prompting this system; the "users" are (a) the deterministic passes immediately downstream within Phase 2 that assemble the Document IR, and (b) — transitively, once the IR is written to disk — every downstream consumer of the canonical Knowledge IR (Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler, and beyond KIR's boundary, any rendering/query/vector-search system a third party builds on top of the IR). "Good" extraction means: every concept/term/entity/reference genuinely present and salient in the document's text is captured (recall), nothing is invented that is not grounded in the document's actual content (precision/faithfulness), and the four categories are not conflated (a glossary term is not duplicated as a generic concept, an entity is not extracted as a reference). Because this pass has no human in the loop at inference time — its output is persisted directly into the canonical IR — "good" must be judged by comparing extraction output against a hand-labeled reference document, not by a downstream user's subjective satisfaction.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Fabricated extraction (low precision / hallucinated content):** The pass invents a concept, glossary term, entity, or reference that does not appear in the source document's text — silently injecting false information into the canonical semantic record, which Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler will then merge across documents and downstream consumers will treat as ground truth with no way to detect the fabrication absent re-reading the raw source.
2. **Silent under-extraction (low recall) on a non-empty document:** The pass returns a near-empty or empty result for a document that genuinely contains extractable concepts/terms/entities/references, and — because D-03 makes this look identical to "the document genuinely has none" once the `output_validator`'s non-empty check is satisfied by even one trivial item — the gap is never surfaced as a diagnostic and the document silently contributes less to the Knowledge IR than it should.
3. **Cross-document leakage:** Any extracted item in one document's output contains information, terminology, or context that did not appear in that document's own sections — violating DOC-02 (Document IR for one document never merges information from another) at the extraction-pass level, before Phase 3's merge logic is even reached.
4. **Category misclassification:** An item is extracted into the wrong one of the four categories (e.g., a glossary term — a defined, reusable piece of terminology — extracted as a free-floating "entity," or a cross-document/external reference extracted as a "concept") — corrupting the semantic shape of the Document IR in a way that is schema-valid (so it passes Pydantic validation) but semantically wrong, making it invisible to code-based checks and only catchable by content-aware evaluation.
5. **Non-deterministic drift across reruns of an unchanged document:** Re-extracting the same document checksum under the same prompt/schema/model version produces a materially different set of concepts/terms/entities/references between runs — undermining the project's core determinism guarantee (PROJECT.md's "Core Value") and breaking the LLM-02 cache's implicit assumption that a cache hit is equivalent to a guaranteed-identical fresh call.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Developer tooling / knowledge engineering infrastructure (horizontal — KIR is explicitly domain-agnostic: it is designed to compile arbitrary Markdown corpora, which in practice may be technical docs, internal wikis, policy docs, or any other text-heavy knowledge base; this phase's extraction pass has no single regulated vertical to anchor it). The relevant "domain" is therefore not a regulated industry but a practice: **structured information extraction / knowledge graph construction from unstructured text**, evaluated the way information-extraction and NLP practitioners evaluate any entity/relation/terminology extraction system.

**User Population:** Two tiers. (1) Direct: the KIR maintainer/operator running `kir compile` against their own corpus (currently a 700-document Slab export per PROJECT.md's Acceptance Corpus) — a technically sophisticated user who can read diagnostics and re-run compiles, but who has no practical way to manually audit hundreds of documents' extraction output line-by-line. (2) Indirect/downstream: any future consumer of the compiled Knowledge IR — Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler (which merges concepts across documents and will propagate this pass's errors into canonical concepts), and beyond KIR's boundary, whatever rendering/query/vector-search system a third party builds on top of the IR. Neither tier reviews extraction output interactively at inference time — there is no human-in-the-loop approval step before an extraction result is persisted into the Document IR.

**Stakes Level:** High. Not "Critical" in the safety/regulatory sense (no PII, medical, financial, or legal decision is made directly from this output), but high because of the project's own stated Core Value: KIR's entire purpose is deterministic, trustworthy compilation where "Knowledge IR... preserves full provenance, and explicitly records (never silently resolves) semantic conflicts" (PROJECT.md). An extraction system that fabricates or silently drops content directly undermines the one thing this product exists to guarantee — and because there is no review gate, errors compound silently across the 700-document corpus and into Phase 3's cross-document merge before any human notices.

**Output Consequence:** Each document's extracted concepts/glossary/entities/references become permanent rows in that document's Document IR YAML artifact (STOR-01) — the canonical semantic record. Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler will treat every extracted item as ground truth input to alias resolution and concept merging (KNOW-01..04); a fabricated concept in one document can merge with and pollute a legitimate canonical concept built from other documents, and a missed concept simply never enters the Knowledge IR at all, with no error to flag its absence (you cannot detect the absence of something that was never recorded). Because LLM-02's cache makes a correct-looking but wrong extraction permanently reproducible (same checksum + prompt + schema + model = same cached wrong answer, forever, until a prompt/schema/model version bump invalidates the cache), a bad extraction is not a transient blip — it becomes the system's stable, idempotent "truth" until someone notices and forces a cache bust.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

```
Dimension: Extraction faithfulness (no fabrication)
Good: Every concept, glossary term, entity, and reference returned can be traced to specific text in the document's sections — a reviewer reading the source can point to the sentence or phrase that justifies the extraction.
Bad: An item appears in the output that has no textual basis in the document — invented, inferred-but-unstated, or hallucinated from general world knowledge about the topic rather than the document's actual content.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Practitioner standard for entity/relation extraction systems — fabrication is the IE-specific analogue of RAG hallucination, and is the single most-cited failure mode in LLM-based knowledge graph construction research (see Known Failure Modes below).

Dimension: Extraction completeness (recall on salient content)
Good: All concepts/terms/entities/references that a careful human reader would flag as worth recording are present in the output — including ones mentioned only once, or defined implicitly through usage rather than an explicit "X is defined as Y" sentence.
Bad: The document clearly discusses or defines several distinct concepts/terms but the extraction returns only one or two of them, or returns a generic restatement of the document's title instead of its actual content-level terms.
Stakes: High
Source: Standard precision/recall framing for structured extraction benchmarks (ExtractBench, 2026) — recall failures are the harder-to-detect half of the precision/recall pair because an under-extracted result still validates against the schema and still passes D-03's "not entirely empty" check.

Dimension: Category correctness (concept vs. glossary vs. entity vs. reference)
Good: Each item is placed in the category matching its actual semantic role in the text — a defined, reusable piece of terminology goes to glossary; a named person/organization/system goes to entities; a pointer to another document/external resource goes to references; a substantive idea or topic goes to concepts.
Bad: A glossary-style defined term is extracted as a generic "concept" (or vice versa), or a reference to another document is extracted as an "entity," collapsing the four-category schema's intended semantic distinctions into noise that Phase 3's per-category merge logic cannot cleanly process.
Stakes: High
Source: Direct consequence of D-02 (one combined output model spans all four categories in a single call) — joint extraction improves recall but raises the specific risk of cross-category confusion that separate per-category calls would not have.

Dimension: Document-scoped grounding (no cross-document leakage)
Good: Every extracted item's content is fully explainable by this document's own sections alone, with zero terminology, names, or facts that originate from a different document the model may have seen during training or via the prompt's few-shot examples.
Bad: An extracted definition or entity description matches knowledge about the topic from outside this specific document's text — most commonly via the model "filling in" a generic/textbook definition for a term the document itself defines differently or more narrowly.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Directly enforces DOC-02 (project requirement: "Document IR for one document never merges information from another") — this is the extraction-pass-level instance of a requirement the project treats as non-negotiable.

Dimension: Reproducibility across reruns (extraction-level determinism)
Good: Re-running extraction on an unchanged document under an unchanged prompt version, schema version, and pinned model id produces the same (or near-identical, accounting for any legitimate non-determinism in the underlying model call) set of extracted items.
Bad: Two runs against the identical cache key produce meaningfully different concept/term/entity/reference sets — e.g., one run extracts 12 concepts, a rerun (cache miss, forced re-call) extracts 4, with little overlap.
Stakes: High
Source: PROJECT.md's stated Core Value — "Given identical raw sources, compiler version, prompt version, and schema version, KIR must deterministically compile raw Markdown into a canonical Knowledge IR" — extraction-level non-determinism breaks this guarantee at its source, before any downstream merge logic runs.
```

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

- **Cumulative error propagation through disjoint pipeline stages.** Knowledge-graph-construction research (LLM-empowered KG construction survey, 2026) documents that when extraction, normalization, and merge are handled as separate disjoint stages — exactly KIR's Document Compiler → Knowledge Compiler split — errors introduced at the extraction stage are not corrected downstream; they accumulate. This phase's extraction pass is the *first* stage in that chain, so any fabrication or omission here is the error budget Phase 3 inherits and cannot recover from, since Phase 3 has no access back to the raw source text to double-check.
- **Silent "gray" errors that never trigger a visible failure.** Research on LLM-based multi-agent/pipeline systems found the large majority of failures (cited at over 75% in one 2026 study) are silent — schema-valid, structurally plausible output that is nevertheless substantively wrong, invisible without manual inspection. This maps precisely onto D-03's failure handling: a `ModelRetry`-exhausted hard failure produces a visible `Diagnostic`, but a fabricated-or-under-extracted result that still satisfies the Pydantic schema and the non-empty `output_validator` produces *no* diagnostic at all — it looks identical to a correct extraction.
- **Prompt-framing brittleness causing inconsistent extraction granularity.** IE/KG-extraction research notes LLMs are sensitive to small prompt-structure and task-framing changes, with extraction answer-sets shifting under near-identical phrasing. Concretely for this pass: without disciplined few-shot examples calibrating "how granular is a concept" (a paragraph-level theme vs. a single noun phrase), extraction granularity can drift between documents processed under the nominally same `prompt_version`, producing an inconsistent Document IR corpus that looks fine document-by-document but merges poorly in Phase 3.
- **Recall asymmetry by content type.** Real-world structured-extraction evaluations (ExtractBench, 2026) report high precision (~90%) but markedly lower, type-dependent recall — some categories of structured items are reliably found while others (in this project's case, plausibly: implicit/undefined-but-used glossary terms, or references embedded in prose rather than markdown links) are reliably missed. The four-category combined schema (D-02) makes this worse if one category systematically "crowds out" attention to another within a single model call.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

None identified for this deployment context. KIR's extraction pass processes arbitrary Markdown text supplied by the operator (technical docs, internal wikis, policy docs per phase_goal) — there is no built-in assumption of PII, PHI, or regulated financial/legal content, and the project's own scope explicitly excludes any rendering, query, or decision-making surface that would trigger downstream regulatory obligations (PROJECT.md Product Boundary: "KIR's public API ends" at the Knowledge IR; everything reaching an end user is out of scope). If a specific operator compiles a corpus containing regulated content (e.g., an internal wiki with PII or PHI), that is a deployment-time data-governance concern for the operator, not a constraint on the extraction pass's design — flagged here only so a future AI-SPEC for a regulated-corpus deployment is not assumed identical to this one.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** PydanticAI

**Version:** >=2.0,<3 (pinned per ARCHITECTURE.md/STACK.md)

**Rationale:**
DOC-03 is a single-shot, combined structured-output extraction call per document (one Pydantic output model returning concepts/glossary/entities/references together, per D-02) — not RAG, not multi-agent orchestration, not a stateful workflow. PydanticAI's `Agent(output_type=DomainModel)` is purpose-built for exactly this shape: provider-agnostic structured output with typed validation and retries (`ModelRetry`/`output_retries`), wrapped behind a single `LLMPort` Protocol method so no pass or domain code ever imports `pydantic_ai` directly. This is the user-confirmed, HIGH-confidence pick already documented in STACK.md and sketched in ARCHITECTURE.md's `PydanticAILLMAdapter`.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Instructor (python-instructor) | Even thinner "wrap any client, get structured output" layer with no agent/tool-calling abstraction — viable if PydanticAI's agent machinery ever proves heavier than this project's single-shot extraction passes need, but not warranted now |
| LangChain / LlamaIndex | Built for RAG/multi-step orchestration; this phase has no retrieval or multi-step workflow, so their abstractions add unneeded surface area and harder LLMPort isolation |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — PydanticAI itself is provider-agnostic (model-string switch across OpenAI/Anthropic/Gemini/etc.), but the codebase is locked into PydanticAI's `Agent`/structured-output API, fully isolated behind `LLMPort` per CLAUDE.md's ports/adapters rule (no LLM SDK imports in `domain/`).

### Entry Point Pattern
```python
# adapters/llm/pydantic_ai_adapter.py
"""Implements core.ports.LLMPort using PydanticAI. Per D-02, one combined
structured-output call per document — concepts/glossary/entities/references
extracted together, not four separate LLMPort calls."""

from __future__ import annotations

from pydantic import BaseModel
from pydantic_ai import Agent, ModelRetry

from kir.core.domain.models.document import Section
from kir.core.ports.llm_port import ExtractionResult, LLMPort  # narrowed per D-02


class ExtractedConceptDTO(BaseModel):
    name: str
    definition: str | None = None
    category: str | None = None


class ExtractedGlossaryTermDTO(BaseModel):
    term: str
    definition: str


class ExtractedEntityDTO(BaseModel):
    name: str
    kind: str  # e.g. "person", "organization", "system" — free text at extraction time


class ExtractedReferenceDTO(BaseModel):
    target: str
    context: str | None = None


class DocumentExtractionOutput(BaseModel):
    """The single combined output_type for the one-call-per-document extraction
    (D-02). All four categories must validate together or the whole call retries."""

    concepts: list[ExtractedConceptDTO] = []
    glossary: list[ExtractedGlossaryTermDTO] = []
    entities: list[ExtractedEntityDTO] = []
    references: list[ExtractedReferenceDTO] = []


class PydanticAIAdapter(LLMPort):
    """The only class in the codebase that constructs a pydantic_ai.Agent.
    Pass code depends on LLMPort, never on this class or on pydantic_ai types
    directly (Anti-Pattern 4, ARCHITECTURE.md)."""

    def __init__(self, model: str, *, prompts_dir, max_output_retries: int = 2):
        self._agent = Agent(
            model,  # e.g. "anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6" or "openai:gpt-5.2" — config value, not code
            output_type=DocumentExtractionOutput,
            retries={"output": max_output_retries},
        )
        self._prompts_dir = prompts_dir

        @self._agent.output_validator
        async def _validate_non_empty_document(output: DocumentExtractionOutput) -> DocumentExtractionOutput:
            # Cheap structural sanity check beyond schema validation — ask the
            # model to retry rather than silently accepting a degenerate result.
            if not any([output.concepts, output.glossary, output.entities, output.references]):
                raise ModelRetry(
                    "Extraction returned nothing in all four categories — "
                    "re-read the document and extract at least one item, or "
                    "confirm the document genuinely has none."
                )
            return output

    async def extract(self, *, sections: list[Section], prompt: str) -> ExtractionResult:
        # async, not run_sync — see Section 4b.2. Caller (the pass) is itself async.
        result = await self._agent.run(prompt)
        return result.output  # NOTE: v2 API — `.output`, not v1's `.data`
```

### Key Abstractions
<!-- Framework-specific concepts the developer must understand before coding -->
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `Agent[DepsT, OutputT]` | Provider-agnostic wrapper binding a model string to an `output_type` and behavior config | Constructed once per `PydanticAIAdapter` instance; never exposed past the adapter boundary (LLMPort is the seam) |
| `output_type=DocumentExtractionOutput` | Declares the Pydantic model the agent must return; drives schema generation + validation | Set at `Agent(...)` construction for the single combined extraction call (D-02) — one model covers concepts+glossary+entities+references |
| `ModelRetry` | Exception raised inside an `@agent.output_validator` (or a tool) to tell the model "this output was invalid, try again" with an explanatory message fed back into the next attempt | Use for semantic validation beyond what Pydantic's schema alone catches (e.g. "extraction must not be entirely empty") |
| `retries={"output": N}` / `output_retries` | Caps how many times PydanticAI will retry on `ModelRetry` or schema validation failure before raising | Set per D-03: after retries exhaust, the adapter must let the failure surface as an exception the pass catches and converts into a `Diagnostic` — never a hard pipeline halt |
| Tool Output mode (default) | `output_type` implemented via tool-calling under the hood — the most broadly provider-compatible structured-output mode | Default; keep it unless a specific provider's tool-calling proves unreliable for this extraction shape, then consider Native Output |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **v1→v2 API renames silently fail, not error loudly.** `result_type` → `output_type`, `.data` → `.output`, `result_retries` → `output_retries`. Because Python doesn't type-check kwargs at call time without a checker running, passing `result_type=` to a v2 `Agent` either raises `TypeError: unexpected keyword argument` (good, loud) or — worse — if copied from a v1 blog post into older AI-generated code, can silently no-op if wrapped in `**kwargs`. Always grep the codebase for `result_type`/`\.data\b`/`result_retries` before merging any PydanticAI code written by inference rather than direct doc lookup.
2. **`Agent.run_sync()` inside an already-running event loop raises `RuntimeError`.** The Document Compiler pipeline and pass execution should be async end-to-end (Section 4b.2) specifically so this is never reached — but if any pass or test harness calls `run_sync()` from inside `pytest-asyncio`'s event loop or an async CLI command, it will crash. Use `await agent.run(...)` everywhere in production code; reserve `run_sync()` for synchronous scripts/REPLs only.
3. **`output_validator` retries cost a full model round-trip each time.** A `ModelRetry` raised from `@agent.output_validator` re-sends context to the model — this is not free. With `retries={"output": 2}` and a flaky prompt, worst case is 3x the per-document LLM cost. Keep validators to genuinely-cheap-to-detect, genuinely-worth-a-retry conditions (e.g. "all four lists empty"), not exhaustive business-rule checks better done as a deterministic post-pass.
4. **`TestModel` generates schema-satisfying garbage, not realistic data — don't use it for golden-fixture regression tests.** Per the official testing docs, "there's no ML or AI in `TestModel`... the resulting data won't look pretty or relevant." Use `TestModel`/`FunctionModel` only for pass-*plumbing* tests (does the pass call `ctx.llm.extract` correctly, handle the returned diagnostics correctly). For LLM-03's golden-fixture corpus (D-04: hand-authored synthetic Markdown + hand-crafted expected output), use `pytest-recording` VCR cassettes (one real recorded call per fixture) or — simpler given hand-crafted expected output is already required — a `FakeLLMAdapter` implementing `LLMPort` directly with canned `DocumentExtractionOutput` values, bypassing PydanticAI entirely for the fixture-replay path.
5. **Forgetting `models.ALLOW_MODEL_REQUESTS = False` in test setup lets a misconfigured test slip through to a live API call.** Set this globally in `conftest.py` (`from pydantic_ai import models; models.ALLOW_MODEL_REQUESTS = False`) so any test that fails to override its `Agent`'s model with `TestModel`/`FunctionModel`/the fake adapter fails loudly and fast instead of burning real API quota in CI — directly enforces this phase's "zero live API calls in CI" requirement (LLM-03).

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
Model is a config value (`Settings.llm_model: str`, e.g. `"anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6"` or `"openai:gpt-5.2"`), never hardcoded in `llm/pydantic_ai_adapter.py` — `model_provider` for this phase is Model-agnostic via PydanticAI's provider-string switch, so the same adapter code runs against any provider PydanticAI supports.
- `temperature`: low (0.0–0.2) via `model_settings=ModelSettings(temperature=0.1)` passed to `Agent(...)` — extraction is a recall/precision task, not creative generation; determinism (CLAUDE.md's decision hierarchy puts Determinism above Performance) favors the lowest temperature that still extracts well.
- `max_tokens`: set explicitly via `model_settings=ModelSettings(max_tokens=4096)` (or higher if synthetic fixture documents prove longer outputs are needed) — never leave unbounded (Section 4b.3).
- `retries={"output": 2}` at `Agent(...)` construction — bounds PydanticAI's own validation-retry loop; this is distinct from any caller-level retry around transient network errors, which is out of scope for the adapter itself.
- **Pinned model id is part of the cache key** (LLM-02: checksum + prompt_version + schema_version + pinned model id) — the adapter must expose the exact resolved model string for the cache-key builder, not just accept it as an opaque constructor arg.

**Core Pattern:**
Single-shot structured extraction per document (D-02) — one `Agent.run()` call returns the combined `DocumentExtractionOutput` (concepts + glossary + entities + references together), driven by the `ExtractConceptsPass` calling `ctx.llm.extract(...)` (the narrow `LLMPort` method), never by pass code touching `pydantic_ai.Agent` directly (Anti-Pattern 4). The pass itself is async (`async def extract_concepts_pass(ir, ctx) -> DocumentIR`) so it can `await ctx.llm.extract(...)` without blocking the pipeline event loop:

```python
# compiler/documents/passes/extract_concepts.py
from kir.core.passes.registry import register_pass
from kir.core.domain.models.document import Document
from kir.core.domain.models.diagnostic import Diagnostic, Severity
from kir.core.passes.context import CompilerContext

@register_pass("extract_concepts", depends_on=("parse", "section", "metadata"))
async def extract_concepts_pass(ir: Document, ctx: CompilerContext) -> Document:
    prompt = ctx.prompts.render("extract_v1", sections=ir.sections)  # Prompt Registry
    cache_key = ctx.llm_cache.build_key(
        checksum=ir.checksum, prompt_version="extract_v1",
        schema_version=ctx.schema_version, model_id=ctx.llm.model_id,
    )
    if cached := ctx.llm_cache.get(cache_key):
        return ir.model_copy(update=_to_document_fields(cached))

    try:
        result = await ctx.llm.extract(sections=ir.sections, prompt=prompt)
    except Exception as exc:  # ModelRetry exhausted / output_retries exceeded (D-03)
        diagnostic = Diagnostic(
            code="extraction-failed", severity=Severity.ERROR,
            message=f"LLM extraction failed after retries: {exc}",
        )
        # D-03: document still produces a Document IR with empty
        # concepts/glossary/entities/references — never a hard pipeline halt.
        return ir.model_copy(update={"diagnostics": (*ir.diagnostics, diagnostic)})

    ctx.llm_cache.set(cache_key, result)
    return ir.model_copy(update=_to_document_fields(result))
```

**Tool Use:**
No agentic tool-calling is needed for this phase — Tool Output mode (PydanticAI's default) is used purely as the structured-output delivery mechanism (the schema is registered as a synthetic "tool" under the hood for broad provider compatibility), not for the agent to invoke real tools mid-run. No `@agent.tool` functions are registered. If a future phase's pass needs the model to call back into the system (e.g. a lookup), that would be a deliberate, separate `LLMPort` method — out of scope for DOC-03/LLM-01.

**State Management:**
Stateless per call — each `Agent.run()` invocation is a single request/response with no conversation history, matching the "one combined call per document" decision (D-02). No `message_history` threading between calls. The only persisted state is:
1. The LLM response cache (`llm/cache.py`, LLM-02) — keyed on checksum+prompt_version+schema_version+model id, built on Phase 1's generic `CachePort`.
2. The resulting `Document` IR itself, persisted via `RepositoryPort` after the pass completes — not by the LLM adapter.
The adapter (`PydanticAIAdapter`) is itself stateless and safe to reuse across documents within a single compiler run (one `Agent` instance constructed once, called per document).

**Context Window Strategy:**
Per CONTEXT.md's phase boundary, this is single-document extraction with no cross-document merging — the only context-window pressure is a single document's sections fitting in one prompt. At the v1/700-doc target scale (per ARCHITECTURE.md's Scaling Considerations), individual documents are not expected to approach typical 128K–200K token context windows. No retrieval, reranking, or cross-document summarization applies here (that's Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler concern, out of scope). If a future pathological document is large enough to risk truncation, the mitigation is a deterministic pre-pass section-budget check (count tokens, split into multiple extraction calls per document) — not built in this phase since no current requirement or fixture demands it; flag as a forward-looking note only.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

PydanticAI's `output_type` parameter on `Agent` *is* the structured-output integration — no separate adapter library (e.g. `instructor`) is needed; this is the framework's core value proposition. For this extraction use case, the output schema is the single combined `DocumentExtractionOutput` model (D-02: one model spans concepts + glossary + entities + references, validated together in one call):

```python
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field

class ExtractedConceptDTO(BaseModel):
    name: str
    definition: str | None = None
    category: str | None = None

class ExtractedGlossaryTermDTO(BaseModel):
    term: str
    definition: str

class ExtractedEntityDTO(BaseModel):
    name: str
    kind: str

class ExtractedReferenceDTO(BaseModel):
    target: str
    context: str | None = None

class DocumentExtractionOutput(BaseModel):
    """output_type for Agent — validated as one unit; partial-category
    failures cannot occur because PydanticAI validates the whole model."""
    concepts: list[ExtractedConceptDTO] = Field(default_factory=list)
    glossary: list[ExtractedGlossaryTermDTO] = Field(default_factory=list)
    entities: list[ExtractedEntityDTO] = Field(default_factory=list)
    references: list[ExtractedReferenceDTO] = Field(default_factory=list)
```

**How the framework integrates:** `Agent(model, output_type=DocumentExtractionOutput)` — PydanticAI generates the JSON schema from the Pydantic model, registers it via Tool Output mode (default — broadest provider compatibility), and validates every response against it automatically. `result.output` (v2 API name; **not** v1's `.data`) is a fully-validated `DocumentExtractionOutput` instance, never a raw dict.

**Retry logic:** `Agent(..., retries={"output": 2})` caps PydanticAI's own retry loop at 2 retries (3 attempts total) on schema-validation failure or a raised `ModelRetry`. Per D-03, retries are PydanticAI's concern only up to that cap — once `output_retries` is exhausted, the adapter lets the exception propagate, and the calling pass (`extract_concepts_pass`) is responsible for catching it and converting it into a `Diagnostic(code="extraction-failed", severity=ERROR, ...)` appended to the Document IR, never a hard pipeline halt. Log at WARNING on each individual `ModelRetry` (cheap, expected occasionally) and at ERROR when the full retry budget is exhausted (signals a systemic prompt/schema/provider problem worth surfacing in CI or monitoring, not just per-document noise).

### Async-First Design

PydanticAI's `Agent.run()` is the async-native entry point; `Agent.run_sync()` is a thin synchronous wrapper that internally drives the same coroutine via `asyncio.run()`. **The one common mistake:** calling `run_sync()` from inside code that is already running on an event loop (an async pass, an async CLI command, a `pytest-asyncio` test) raises `RuntimeError: asyncio.run() cannot be called from a running event loop`. Because this project's pass pipeline is itself async end-to-end (the `extract_concepts_pass` in Section 4 is `async def`), every call into `PydanticAIAdapter.extract()` must use `await self._agent.run(prompt)` — never `run_sync()` — so the adapter composes correctly inside the pipeline's event loop and inside `pytest-asyncio` tests without ever hitting this trap. Reserve `run_sync()` exclusively for throwaway scripts/REPL exploration outside the compiler pipeline.

**Stream vs. await:** This extraction use case has no UI consuming partial output — there is no end user watching tokens arrive — so `agent.run_stream()` is not used here; `await agent.run(...)` is correct because the only thing that matters is the fully-validated `DocumentExtractionOutput`, and partial/unvalidated JSON mid-stream is not actionable for a structured-output-only batch compiler pass. Streaming would only become relevant if a future interactive surface (explicitly out of scope per PROJECT.md's product boundary) needed to show extraction progress live.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

**System vs. user prompt separation:** Use PydanticAI's `instructions=` (or `system_prompt=` for persistent agent identity) to carry the fixed extraction contract — "you are extracting concepts/glossary/entities/references from a technical document; respond only via the structured output schema; do not invent information not present in the text" — and reserve the per-call `agent.run(prompt)` user-turn content for the actual document sections being extracted. This keeps the *instructions* (versioned alongside the Prompt Registry's `prompt_version`) cleanly separable from the *data* (the document content, which varies per call and must never be cached as part of the prompt template).

**Few-shot:** Inline, not dynamically retrieved — this phase has no retrieval pass or example database (RAG is explicitly out of scope per PROJECT.md), so any few-shot examples illustrating desired extraction granularity (e.g. "a glossary term has a one-sentence definition, not a paragraph") are baked directly into the versioned prompt template under `llm/prompts/extract_v1.md`, content-addressed by `prompt_version` exactly like the instructions themselves — changing the examples is a prompt-version bump, not a runtime parameter.

**`max_tokens`:** Set explicitly via `model_settings=ModelSettings(max_tokens=4096)` on the `Agent` (Section 4) — never left unbounded. Unbounded `max_tokens` in production risks runaway cost on a pathological document and makes the cost budget below unverifiable; pick a number generous enough for the largest hand-authored fixture document's expected combined extraction output plus headroom, and treat hitting the cap as a `Diagnostic`-worthy signal (truncated structured output will fail Pydantic validation and trigger the same `ModelRetry`/D-03 path).

### Context Window Management

This system type (Extraction, single-document, no cross-document merge per the phase boundary) has no RAG-style reranking/truncation concern and no multi-turn conversation to summarize — there is exactly one user turn per call (the document's sections) and zero message history carried between calls (Section 4's State Management). The relevant context-window discipline is therefore narrower than RAG/Conversational/Autonomous patterns: ensure a single document's rendered sections plus the fixed instructions/few-shot prompt fit within the chosen model's context window. At v1/700-doc scale (ARCHITECTURE.md), no document is expected to approach typical context limits; if a future document does, the correct fix is a deterministic pre-pass token-budget check that splits the document into multiple extraction calls (each producing a partial `DocumentExtractionOutput` merged deterministically afterward) — not built now, flagged only as a forward-looking note since no current fixture or requirement demands it.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-call cost estimate:** One combined extraction call per document (D-02) at the v1/700-document corpus target means **at most 700 LLM calls for a full from-scratch compile**, not 700×4 (the combined-call decision is explicitly the cost-reduction lever here vs. four separate `LLMPort` calls). Concretely budget per call as: prompt tokens (instructions + few-shot + one document's sections, typically low thousands of tokens for hand-authored-scale documents) + completion tokens bounded by `max_tokens=4096`. Multiply provider-specific per-token pricing by ~700 to get a full-corpus ceiling; track this number in production monitoring (Section 7) once a concrete provider/model is locked at implementation time.

**Caching:** LLM-02's required response cache (keyed on document checksum + prompt_version + schema_version + pinned model id, `llm/cache.py` built on Phase 1's `CachePort`) **is** this system's exact-match cache — a recompile of an unchanged document under an unchanged prompt/schema/model never re-calls the LLM at all, making re-runs free and fast by construction. No semantic/fuzzy caching layer is warranted on top of this: extraction prompts are not natural-language queries with paraphrase variance, they're deterministic renders of a document's sections, so exact-match on the cache key is sufficient and strictly more correct (no risk of serving a near-match result for a document that actually changed).

**Sub-task model routing:** Not applicable in this phase — there is exactly one LLM-backed task (combined extraction), no classification/routing/summarization sub-tasks to offload to a cheaper model. This becomes relevant in Phase 3's Knowledge Compiler (`resolve_aliases`, `build_relations`, `build_taxonomy` are separate LLM-backed passes that could plausibly use different model tiers) but is out of this phase's scope — noted here only so the pattern isn't lost when that phase's AI-SPEC is written.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> System shape note: this is Extraction-type with no end-user-facing runtime — the "system" is a batch CLI compiler pass, not a served model behind live traffic. There is no RAG retrieval (no context faithfulness/citation dimensions) and no agent loop (no task-decomposition/tool-use dimensions). The five dimensions below are inherited directly from Section 1b's domain rubric ingredients, which were already written in measurable PASS/FAIL form by `gsd-domain-researcher` — this section adds measurement approach, priority, and tooling on top of that existing rubric rather than re-deriving new dimensions.

### Dimensions

| Dimension | Rubric (Pass/Fail) | Measurement Approach | Priority |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Extraction faithfulness (no fabrication)** | PASS: Every concept/glossary term/entity/reference in the output can be traced to a specific sentence or phrase in the document's own sections. FAIL: An item appears with no textual basis in the document — invented, inferred-but-unstated, or filled in from general world knowledge about the topic. | LLM Judge (grounding check against source sections) + Human (calibration sample) | Critical |
| **Extraction completeness (recall)** | PASS: All concepts/terms/entities/references a careful human reader would flag as worth recording are present, including single-mention or implicitly-defined ones. FAIL: The document clearly discusses several distinct items but only one or two are returned, or the output is a generic restatement of the title rather than content-level terms. | Code (set-overlap recall@golden-fixture) + Human (real-corpus spot-check, since recall failures are invisible to schema/non-empty checks per Section 1b) | High |
| **Category correctness (concept vs. glossary vs. entity vs. reference)** | PASS: Each item's category matches its actual semantic role — defined reusable terminology → glossary; named person/org/system → entities; pointer to another doc/external resource → references; substantive idea/topic → concepts. FAIL: A glossary-style defined term lands in concepts (or vice versa), or a reference is extracted as an entity. | LLM Judge (rubric-based category check) + Human (calibration) | High |
| **Document-scoped grounding (no cross-document leakage)** | PASS: Every extracted item's content is fully explainable by this document's own sections, with zero terminology/names/facts originating elsewhere. FAIL: An extracted definition/entity matches a generic/textbook understanding of the topic that diverges from how *this* document actually defines or uses it. | LLM Judge (cross-reference against full source text, not other documents) + Code (no-op structurally, but enforced indirectly via single-document-only prompt context per Section 4) | Critical |
| **Reproducibility across reruns (extraction-level determinism)** | PASS: Re-running extraction on an unchanged document under unchanged prompt/schema/model-id cache key produces the same (or near-identical) item set. FAIL: Two runs against the identical cache key produce meaningfully different concept/term/entity/reference sets. | Code (golden-fixture exact-match replay in CI; cache-key equality test) | High |
| **Schema/output validity** *(baseline, not in Section 1b — added because it gates all four content dimensions above)* | PASS: Output validates as `DocumentExtractionOutput` and satisfies the non-empty `output_validator` (at least one item across the four categories, or — for empty fixtures — an explicit "genuinely empty" golden label). FAIL: Pydantic validation fails after `output_retries` exhausted, or output passes schema but is the degenerate "all four lists empty" case on a document with known extractable content. | Code (Pydantic validation, exercised automatically by every golden-fixture test) | Critical |

**Why no LLM-judge-only dimension stands alone:** every LLM Judge row above is paired with a Code or Human check because Section 1b's "Known Failure Modes" explicitly flags this domain's silent-gray-error risk (>75% of pipeline failures are schema-valid but substantively wrong) — an uncalibrated judge would itself be vulnerable to the same fabrication/under-extraction failure modes it is meant to catch.

### Eval Tooling

**Primary Tool:** Promptfoo (CLI-first, no platform account, matches the project's no-hidden-infra/no-global-state philosophy) + code-based Pydantic validators, per `ai-evals.md`'s "Structured extraction → Promptfoo + code-based validators" mapping. No tracing platform (Phoenix/Langfuse/LangSmith) is adopted for this phase — see rationale below.

**Tracing platform scan result:** `grep` across `*.py`/`*.ts`/`*.toml`/`*.json` for `langfuse|langsmith|arize|phoenix|braintrust|promptfoo|ragas` found no matches anywhere in the repo. No existing tool to inherit; defaults below are opinionated picks, not detections.

**Why not Arize Phoenix (the default per the planner's standing instructions):** Phoenix is an observability/tracing platform for *live, served* systems with ongoing request traffic. This phase's "system" is a batch CLI compiler pass invoked per `kir compile` run, with the LLM-02 cache making most reruns not call the LLM at all — there is no live traffic to trace, and standing up an OpenTelemetry collector + Phoenix server for a project whose explicit architecture principle is "no hidden side effects, no global state" (CLAUDE.md) would add operational surface area disproportionate to this phase's actual eval need. RAGAS is similarly not adopted — RAGAS's metrics (faithfulness, answer relevance, context precision/recall) are purpose-built for RAG retrieval-then-generate pipelines; this phase has no retrieval step (RAG is explicitly out of scope per PROJECT.md), so RAGAS's metric set does not map onto a single-shot structured-extraction call. **Composition:** Per D-04 (small, hand-authored synthetic Markdown, not real corpus excerpts), the 10 starting fixtures should cover:

- 2 documents exercising **category-boundary ambiguity** deliberately — a term that could plausibly be glossary-or-concept, an entity-vs-reference edge case — to pin down category-correctness behavior (Section 1b's #3 failure mode)
- 2 documents with **implicit/undefined-but-used terminology** (a term used several times but never given an explicit "X is defined as Y" sentence) — targets the recall-asymmetry failure mode from Section 1b's Known Failure Modes
- 1 document that is **genuinely sparse** (one short section, maybe one extractable concept) — distinguishes "correctly near-empty" from silent under-extraction (Section 1b's failure mode #2)
- 1 document with content **adjacent to well-known public terminology** (e.g. a doc that redefines a common term narrowly) — targets the cross-document-leakage / world-knowledge-bleed failure mode (Section 1b's #3 and #4)
- 1-2 documents with multiple headings at mixed levels (H1–H6 per D-01) to exercise extraction across an irregular section structure, not just a single H2-per-topic layout

**Creation timeline:** Fixtures are authored during DOC-03 implementation (alongside the pass and the real `PydanticAIAdapter`), not after — LLM-03 already requires the golden-fixture corpus to exist before any pass code can be unit-tested, so dataset and implementation are necessarily concurrent.

## 6. Guardrails

> "Online/real-time" here means "runs synchronously inside every `kir compile` invocation, before the Document IR is persisted" — there is no separate user-facing request path to distinguish from a background job; the compile run itself is the one and only execution path for this pass.

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Schema/structural validation | Every extraction call's response is validated against `DocumentExtractionOutput` by PydanticAI automatically | Block — PydanticAI retries internally (`output_retries`); on exhaustion the adapter raises and the pass catches it, converting to a `Diagnostic(code="extraction-failed", severity=ERROR)` per D-03 — never persists invalid structured output |
| Non-empty output check (`@agent.output_validator`) | Output has zero items across all four categories (concepts/glossary/entities/references) | Flag via `ModelRetry` — asks the model to re-read and retry once before accepting (cap: `retries={"output": 2}` per Section 4); guards against the most catastrophic form of silent under-extraction (a fully-empty result for a clearly non-empty document) without adding a second LLM round-trip beyond what D-03 already budgets |
| Cache-key construction integrity | Before any cache read/write, `checksum + prompt_version + schema_version + model_id` must all be present and non-empty | Block — a malformed cache key (e.g. missing pinned model id) must fail loudly rather than silently caching under a degenerate/partial key, which would defeat LLM-02's reproducibility guarantee |

**Deliberately not made an online guardrail:** faithfulness, category-correctness, and cross-document-grounding checks (Section 5's LLM-judge dimensions) are *not* run synchronously inside every compile. Per `ai-evals.md`'s guardrail-vs-flywheel test ("would this be catastrophic for the business if it goes wrong, right now, in real time") — a single document's fabricated concept is serious but not catastrophic in the way a corrupted cache key or an unvalidated schema write would be, and these checks require either a second LLM call (judge) or human review, both too slow/costly to run on every one of ~700 documents per compile. They are offline flywheel signals instead.

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Extraction faithfulness (LLM-judge fabrication rate) | Run the LLM judge against the full 10-20-document golden-fixture corpus on every prompt_version bump; sample N real-corpus documents (start: 20, post-Phase-2 per Section 1b's "Senior practitioner reviewing real-corpus samples" role) on a periodic/manual cadence once the real 700-doc corpus is available | If fabrication rate on golden fixtures exceeds 0 (any fabrication on a known-answer fixture is a regression) → block the prompt_version bump from merging. If real-corpus sampled fabrication rate trends upward across two consecutive manual reviews → escalate to human review of the full sample and consider a prompt revision |
| Extraction completeness (recall@golden-fixture) | Code-based recall computed against all golden fixtures on every CI run (cheap, deterministic, no LLM judge needed since golden labels exist) | If recall on any fixture drops below its previously-passing baseline → CI failure, block merge. This is the cheapest, highest-signal check in the whole eval suite and should gate every prompt/schema change |
| Category-correctness confusion rate | LLM judge + human spot-check, same cadence as faithfulness (golden fixtures every prompt_version bump; periodic real-corpus sample) | Sustained confusion on a specific category pair (e.g. glossary↔concept) → revise few-shot examples in `extract_v1.md` (a prompt_version bump, per Section 4b's prompt-versioning discipline), not a silent runtime workaround |
| Reproducibility / determinism drift | Code-based: rerun the full golden-fixture corpus twice under the identical cache key and diff results, on every CI run | Any non-identical rerun result under an unchanged cache key is treated as Critical — per Section 1b's failure mode #5, this breaks the project's Core Value and should fail CI immediately, not just log a warning |
| Per-document LLM cost/token usage | Code-based: log token counts per extraction call (available from PydanticAI's response) during real-corpus compile runs (post-Phase-2, when the real corpus is available) | If average tokens-per-document trends significantly above the Section 4b cost-budget estimate → investigate prompt bloat or document-size outliers before they silently inflate the ~700-call full-compile cost ceiling |

## 7. Production Monitoring

> "Production" for this phase means **CI + on-demand `kir compile` runs against the real corpus**, not a served application with live user traffic — there is no request-response server, no uptime SLA, and no concurrent-user load to monitor. The closest analogue to "production monitoring" is: did the last compile run produce diagnostics worth a human looking at, and is extraction quality holding steady across prompt/schema/model changes over time.

**Tracing Tool:** None adopted as a hosted/self-hosted platform (see Section 5 rationale: no live traffic to justify Phoenix/Langfuse/LangSmith operational overhead in this phase). Instead:
- **Structured logging** at WARNING (each individual `ModelRetry`) and ERROR (retry budget exhausted) per Section 4b's retry-logic guidance — surfaced through the existing `Diagnostic` mechanism (CORE-06) that every pass already returns, so extraction-pass observability rides the same structured-diagnostics pipeline as every other pass rather than a bespoke side channel (consistent with CLAUDE.md's "no hidden side effects" principle).
- **Forward-looking note:** if a future phase introduces a long-running/served surface (explicitly out of scope per PROJECT.md's Product Boundary for this project as a whole), Arize Phoenix is the right default to revisit at that time — this is a phase-scoped decision, not a permanent rejection of tracing tooling for the project.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Diagnostic count/severity per compile run** — count of `code="extraction-failed"` diagnostics (D-03 hard-failure path) per `kir compile` invocation; a non-zero count on a previously-clean corpus is the first signal something regressed.
2. **Cache hit rate** — fraction of documents served from the LLM-02 cache vs. fresh LLM calls per compile run; an unexpectedly low hit rate on an otherwise-unchanged corpus indicates a cache-key construction bug (e.g. prompt/schema/model id drifting unintentionally) rather than genuine document changes.
3. **Recall@golden-fixture (CI-gated)** — tracked over time across prompt_version bumps, not just pass/fail at a point in time, to catch slow degradation a single CI run wouldn't flag as a hard failure.
4. **Per-document token usage / estimated cost** — per Section 4b's cost-budget discussion; tracked on real-corpus compile runs once available, to catch prompt bloat before it silently inflates the ~700-document full-compile cost ceiling.
5. **Category-distribution shape** — total counts of concepts/glossary/entities/references extracted per compile run across the corpus; a sudden shift in the relative proportions (e.g. entities count collapsing to near-zero) is a cheap, code-based proxy signal for category-misclassification drift, worth checking before a full LLM-judge re-run.

**Alert Thresholds:**
- Any `extraction-failed` diagnostic on a fixture or real-corpus document that previously compiled cleanly → fails CI / flagged for immediate review (Critical failure mode #1/#3, no silent tolerance).
- Recall@golden-fixture drops below its last-passing baseline on any fixture → blocks merge (see Section 6 offline flywheel table).
- Cache hit rate on an unchanged real corpus drops by more than a small margin (e.g. more than a handful of unexpected cache misses) run-over-run → investigate cache-key construction before trusting the compile's cost/determinism guarantees.
- Two consecutive non-identical reruns under an identical cache key (determinism check, Section 6) → Critical, blocks merge immediately, never just a warning.

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
Per `ai-evals.md`'s "weight toward interactions with concerning signals" guidance, adapted to a batch-compile context (no individual user interactions to weight): prioritize human/LLM-judge review of documents that surface a *signal* during an otherwise-automated compile run, specifically —
- Any document that triggered a `ModelRetry` (even if it eventually succeeded) — these are the documents most likely to be borderline cases for faithfulness/completeness even though they passed schema validation.
- Documents whose extracted item count is a statistical outlier (far above or, especially, far below the corpus median) — the cheapest available proxy for both fabrication (outlier-high) and under-extraction (outlier-low).
- A fixed periodic random sample (e.g. 20 documents) from the real 700-document corpus once available, reviewed by the "Senior practitioner reviewing real-corpus samples" role defined in Section 1b — this is the only mechanism that can surface recall/category-confusion failure modes specific to the real corpus's actual writing style, since the synthetic golden-fixture corpus by design cannot.
